Definitions & Caveats

  • What's included: Hospital inpatient discharges — patients formally admitted to a hospital bed. Includes patients admitted via the emergency department.
  • What's NOT included: ER treat-and-release visits, where a patient was seen in the ER but not admitted. The majority of injury-related ER visits fall here. No public NYC dataset with diagnosis-level detail exists for these visits.
  • The 2017–2018 jump: Data before 2018 uses CCS diagnosis codes (20 injury categories); 2018 onward uses CCSR codes (70+ categories). CCSR captures injuries more broadly, inflating post-2017 totals. Trends within each era are reliable; cross-era comparisons require caution.
  • Borough: Based on hospital location, not patient residence. A Bronx resident treated at a Manhattan hospital is counted under Manhattan.
  • Age: Pre-grouped (0–17, 18–29, 30–49, 50–69, 70+) for patient privacy.
  • Complications: Device/implant and surgical/medical complication categories represent injuries from medical treatment, distinct from traumatic injuries.
  • Counting: Each record = one discharge, not one person. A patient hospitalized twice counts twice.
Source: SPARCS Hospital Inpatient Discharges (De-Identified), NY State DOH, 2009–2024. All data de-identified per HIPAA.

— NYC Family Dining Costs —

What Does Dinner
Actually Cost?

We checked menus at 500 neighborhood restaurants across all five boroughs to find out what a family of four actually pays for a weeknight dinner in New York City — no tablecloths, no tasting menus, just the places where people eat.

How we calculated: Two adult entrées, two kid meals (or smaller portions where no kids' menu exists), one shared appetizer or side, and four drinks (sodas or juice). Tax included at 8.875%. Standard 18% tip included. No alcohol, no dessert.

How Does This Compare?

Metro Area Household Dining Spend
Households in the NY-Newark-Jersey City metro spent $4,679/year on food away from home in 2023–24 — about 40% of their total food budget.
Source: BLS Consumer Expenditure Survey, NY-Newark-Jersey City metro
Restaurant Price Inflation
Food-away-from-home prices in the NY-Newark-Jersey City metro rose 3.5% from February 2025 to February 2026 — outpacing overall inflation.
Source: BLS Consumer Price Index, NY-Newark-Jersey City metro
Cooking at Home Instead?
The USDA's moderate grocery plan for a family of four runs $1,250–$1,400/month — roughly $42–$47/day for all meals.
Source: USDA Food Plans, 2025

Prices gathered March 2026 from publicly available in-restaurant menus and restaurant websites (not delivery platform prices, which are typically 15–30% higher). Estimates assume standard portions at menu price, NYC sales tax of 8.875%, and a standard 18% tip on the pre-tax subtotal. Prices are re-checked monthly; trend data appears after the first update. Individual experiences will vary. This is not a review or recommendation — it's a price check.

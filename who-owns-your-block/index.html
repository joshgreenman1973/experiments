Who Owns Your Block

A map of property ownership concentration across New York City

In New York City, a single LLC can quietly control an entire block\u2019s worth of housing. Shell companies obscure who truly owns buildings, making it nearly impossible for tenants and neighbors to know who\u2019s making decisions about their homes. This map reveals ownership concentration at the tax-block level\u2014showing where property is held by many distinct owners and where a handful of entities control everything. The more concentrated a block\u2019s ownership, the more power rests in fewer hands.

Methodology & Data

This map uses data from NYC\u2019s Primary Land Use Tax Lot Output (PLUTO), published by the Department of City Planning via the NYC Open Data portal. Ownership concentration is calculated at the tax-block level: for each block, we count the number of distinct owners listed across all lots. The \u201cconcentration score\u201d represents the percentage of a block\u2019s lots controlled by the single largest owner. Blocks where one entity owns every lot score 100%; blocks with many different owners score lower. LLC names are taken directly from property records and may obscure the ultimate beneficial owner. Assessed values reflect the city\u2019s most recent tax assessment and may differ from market value.
